1. Is SDA the right archive?
Assess whether SDA is the right place for your data. The SDA can only take certain types of data and the data submitted to the repository must require controlled access. Read about how to find out if SDA is suitable or not.
2. Create a submission request
The SDA is under development and only able to handle a limited number of submission requests. If you think that SDA is the right place for your data and want to be added to our waiting list, please fill in the SDA submission request webform. SDA Helpdesk will contact you as soon as we are ready to start working on your submission.
3. Get a submission account at Central EGA
Once you receive a confirmation from the SDA Helpdesk to proceed with your submission, you need to request an account at Central EGA. Send an email to the EGA Helpdesk and ask for an account for submitting data to the Swedish node of the Federated EGA. Using the received credentials, you should be able to log in to the EGA Submitter Portal.
4. Register Study, Sample, DAC and Policy
Use the EGA Submitter Portal to register your Study, Samples, Data Access Committee (DAC) and Policy (with Data Access Agreement [DAA]).
5. Encrypt and upload data
Follow the SDA-specific instructions on GitHub on how to encrypt files, calculate checksums and upload data.
6. Register experiments and runs
Associate each data file with a registered sample and study by linking files to samples in the EGA Submitter Portal. Details of the experimental procedure you followed must also be provided.
7. Finish your submission
Group your runs/analysis into datasets and link them to your new or existing Data Access Committee (DAC) and Data Access Agreement (DAA) using the EGA Submitter Portal. Data requests are done at a dataset level, thus files within a dataset must share release conditions.
8. Release the data
Instruct the SDA Helpdesk to release your study. All registered studies are automatically placed on hold until the named submission or Data Access Committee (DAC) contact instructs the SDA Helpdesk for the study to be released.
