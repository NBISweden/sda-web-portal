1. Is SDA the right archive?
Assess whether SDA is the right place for your data. The repository is for sharing sensitive human data that requires controlled access, which means that formal applications must be made to access submitted files. Furthermore, only certain data types are accepted. Learn more about how to assess whether SDA is suitable or not.
2. Create a submission request
The SDA is under development and can currently only handle a limited number of submission requests. If you consider SDA to be the right place for your data and want to be added to our waiting list, please fill in the SDA submission request webform. The SDA Helpdesk will evaluate your request and get back to you regarding how to proceed.
3. Receive credentials for a submission account at Central EGA
After you have received a confirmation from the SDA Helpdesk to proceed with your submission, SDA Helpdesk will request a submission account for you at Central EGA. Using the received credentials, you should be able to log in to the EGA Submitter Portal.
4. Register Study, Sample, DAC and Policy
Use the EGA Submitter Portal to register your Study, Samples, Data Access Committee (DAC) and Policy (with Data Access Agreement [DAA]).
5. Encrypt and upload data
Follow the SDA-specific instructions on GitHub on how to encrypt files, calculate checksums and upload data.
6. Register Experiments and Runs
Associate each data file with a registered sample and study by linking files to samples in the EGA Submitter Portal. Details of the experimental procedure you followed must be provided.
7. Finish your submission
Group your runs/analysis into datasets and link them to your new or existing Data Access Committee (DAC) and Data Access Agreement (DAA) using the EGA Submitter Portal. Data requests are done at a dataset level, thus files within a dataset must share release conditions.
8. Release the data
Instruct the SDA Helpdesk to release your study. All registered studies are automatically placed on hold until the named submission or Data Access Committee (DAC) contact instructs the SDA Helpdesk for the study to be released.
